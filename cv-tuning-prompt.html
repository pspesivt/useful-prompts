CV Tuning Prompt

A single, reusable prompt that takes your CV and a target job description and returns a fine-tuned CV designed to pass both ATS keyword screens and a recruiter's six-second scan — without fabrication.

Domain-agnostic TechHealthcareSalesFinanceLegalAcademiaCreativeTradesExecutiveEducationNonprofitGovernmentRetail

Quick start

  1. Open Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or any frontier LLM.
  2. Paste the prompt on the next page.
  3. Replace <paste candidate's current CV verbatim> with your CV.
  4. Replace <paste target job description verbatim> with the JD.
  5. Send. Read the output: the CV, then VERIFICATION QUEUE (if any), then FIT BRIEF — DO NOT SUBMIT.
  6. Resolve each [ ] verify: item, read the Fit Brief carefully, then delete both diagnostic sections before submitting.

The prompt

Paste verbatim into your LLM of choice 
ROLE
Rewrite the candidate's CV to maximize their chance of passing both ATS keyword
screens and a recruiter's 6-second scan, for the supplied job description.

INPUTS
[CV]: <paste candidate's current CV verbatim>
[JD]: <paste target job description verbatim>

TRUTH BOUNDARY
- Use only facts present in [CV]. Do not invent or upgrade employers, titles,
  dates, degrees, licenses, certifications, clients, tools, metrics, scope,
  headcount, budgets, ownership, or outcomes.
- Date precision matches source. If [CV] gives only a year, output only the
  year. Never invent months.
- Bound every achievement to the candidate's actual radius of ownership. Team
  contributions stay contributions ("Wrote broadcast copy for a $10M national
  campaign"), never become ownership claims ("Led $10M campaign").
- Use [JD]'s exact phrasing (both noun and verb forms where [JD] uses both)
  ONLY where [CV] genuinely supports the underlying claim. Where evidence is
  partial, use the noun form with truthful scope ("Exposure to P&L"); skip the
  verb form.
- Prefer active verbs (directed, built, secured, treated, taught, closed).
  Preserve "supported / assisted / contributed to" where they truthfully
  describe a collaborative role. Never escalate "supported" to "managed."

INFERENCE MARKER
If a metric, scope, or credential is plausibly implied by [CV] but not stated,
do NOT insert it inline — ATS regex can mangle inline brackets and trigger
falsification flags. Instead append a section titled `VERIFICATION QUEUE`
after the CV, listing each inferred item as: `[ ] verify: <claim> — implied
by: <source CV line>`. The candidate confirms or removes before submitting.

FIT BRIEF (for the candidate's eyes only)
After the CV (and after the VERIFICATION QUEUE if present), append a final
section titled `FIT BRIEF — DO NOT SUBMIT`. This is a candid, brutally honest
diagnosis to help the candidate decide whether to apply and how to position.

- `Strengths` (3–7 bullets): where [CV] genuinely meets or exceeds a [JD]
  requirement. Each bullet: `<JD requirement> ← <CV evidence: employer,
  scope, metric, credential, or named project>`. Rank by how load-bearing
  the strength is for THIS JD, not by how impressive it sounds in isolation.
- `Gaps` (3–7 bullets): where [JD] asks for something [CV] does not
  demonstrate, or demonstrates only weakly. Tag each: `[blocker]` (explicit
  hard requirement — e.g., active license, "5+ years", named tool),
  `[preferred]` (stated as preferred / strongly desired), or `[nice]`
  (mentioned but not weighted). For each gap, append one of:
  `closeable: <specific action>` (a course, cert, sample project, or
  reframing the candidate can do in days or weeks) or `structural` (years
  of experience, regulated credential, industry-specific tenure — not
  closeable before this application).
- `Overall fit`: one verdict — `strong fit`, `stretch`, `long shot`, or
  `wrong target` — followed by one sentence of reasoning grounded in the
  Strengths and Gaps above.
- `Positioning`: 1–2 sentences on how to open the cover letter or the
  first interview moment to neutralize the top-ranked gap. Concrete
  phrasing the candidate can adapt directly.

This section is diagnostic only. The candidate deletes it entirely before
submitting the CV. Never place any Fit Brief content inside the CV body.

STRUCTURE (ATS-parseable + recruiter-scannable)
- Plain text. Single column. Reverse-chronological. No tables, columns,
  graphics, icons, page headers, or footers.
- Section headers, exact and in this order (omit any with no content):
  `Summary`, `Skills`, `Experience`, `Education`, `Certifications`, `Licenses`.
- Top of page 1:
    1. Candidate name.
    2. Headline using [JD]'s role title verbatim if [CV] truthfully supports
       it (no implied license, credential, or seniority); else the closest
       truthful target-aligned headline still containing JD-relevant keywords.
    3. 2–3 line Summary naming the candidate's strongest recognizable
       employers, clients, credentials, or schools, plus ONE candidate-owned
       quantified achievement relevant to [JD]. No `[verify]` markers here.
- Each Experience entry header on one atomic line:
  `Job Title | Employer | City, ST | MM/YYYY – MM/YYYY` (use `Present` for
  current; use `YYYY – YYYY` if only years are in [CV]).
- First bullet of every role = scope baseline drawn from [CV]: headcount,
  budget, caseload, territory, store count, square footage, patient volume,
  jurisdiction, or comparable scale.
- Subsequent bullets: past-tense verb → [JD]-relevant noun → directional
  outcome where stated, named context or scope where not. ≤ 5 bullets per
  role, ≤ 2 lines each. Cut "responsible for," "duties included."
- Skills: comma-separated under category labels (e.g.,
  `Clinical Systems: Epic, Cerner, Meditech`). Spell out acronyms on first
  use as `Acronym (Full Expansion)` — only where truthful (e.g., RN, CPA,
  OSHA 30, PMP).
- Account for gaps > 4 months and tenures < 12 months with one truthful
  framing line (contract, relocation, layoff, caregiving, licensure pursuit,
  sabbatical). Silence reads worse than the gap.

KEYWORD DISCIPLINE
Surface every [JD] term the candidate genuinely demonstrates — in the Skills
block AND in matching Experience bullets (raw frequency matters for ATS
scoring). Drop, demote, or compress bullets that are generic, outdated, or
weakly related to [JD], even if impressive elsewhere.

OUTPUT
Return, in this exact order: (1) the rewritten CV, (2) `VERIFICATION QUEUE`
if any inferred items exist, (3) `FIT BRIEF — DO NOT SUBMIT` (always).
No preamble, no commentary, no diff between sections.

Usage guidelines

Preparing your inputs

Your CV. Paste it as verbatim text. Avoid screenshots or PDFs — the model needs to read every word. If your CV is in a complex layout (tables, columns, sidebars), copy the text content only; the prompt will produce a clean ATS-parseable structure regardless of what your source looks like.

The job description. Paste the full text from the posting. Don't summarize — the prompt deconstructs the JD into must-haves, nice-to-haves, implicit competencies, and recurring vocabulary, then mirrors only where your experience genuinely supports it. Truncation costs you keyword hits.

Working with the Verification Queue

The output ends with a VERIFICATION QUEUE listing inferred claims the model couldn't confirm from your CV.

Do not submit the CV with verification items unresolved. Treat the queue as a checklist between the model's draft and a real recruiter's inbox.
Confirm Replace the inferred wording with your actual number, scope, or credential. When The claim is true and you can back it up.
Soften Keep the bullet but remove the unverified number or scope. When Directionally true, but you don't have the exact figure.
Remove Delete the bullet entirely. When The claim isn't true, or you can't defend it in interview.

After resolving, delete the VERIFICATION QUEUE section before submitting.

Reading the Fit Brief

The Fit Brief is the only section the model writes for you rather than for the recruiter. Expect a candid, brutally honest read — flattery would defeat the point. Treat it as a five-minute coaching session: calibrate your effort before you spend hours polishing a cover letter for the wrong role.

strong fit Real, repeated evidence for the core requirements. Do Apply now. Polish the cover letter around your top two Strengths.
stretch Most boxes checked; one or two real gaps you can close fast. Do Spend a day on the top closeable gap, then apply.
long shot Multiple gaps; at least one is structural. Do Apply only if the role is exceptional and you accept low odds.
wrong target Core requirements aren't there. A cover letter is unlikely to close the gap. Do Look for a closer-fit JD before investing time on this one.
Strengths
Your cover-letter opener and first-interview anchors. Pick the top one or two; prepare a 60-second story for each with concrete numbers.
Gaps
Triage by tag. Every [blocker] is a real risk; if any are structural, reconsider applying. Every closeable gap becomes a one-week prep list — take the course, build the sample, get the recommendation, reframe the bullet.
Overall fit
A one-word verdict (see strip above) that calibrates how much effort to invest. Take it seriously — the model is more willing than most recruiters to call a stretch a stretch.
Positioning
A literal opener you can adapt for the cover letter or first interview answer. Drafted specifically to defuse your single biggest objection — use it.

Reading the queue length — secondary signal

Short queue (0–3 items): Your CV substantively matches what the JD asks. Resolve the queue, read the Fit Brief, submit.
Long queue (8+ items): The model couldn't anchor much of your language to your record. Combined with a stretch or worse verdict above, this is a clear signal — close gaps before applying, or pick a better-fit JD.

What this prompt does not do

  • Fabricate credentials, titles, dates, employers, metrics, or ownership claims.
  • Escalate "supported" to "managed" or "team member" to "team lead."
  • Keyword-stuff JD vocabulary your CV doesn't back up.
  • Produce a cover letter, LinkedIn rewrite, or interview prep — only the CV.
  • Hide employment gaps or short tenures — it surfaces them with one truthful framing line each.
  • Decide for you whether to apply — the Fit Brief is a mirror, not a hiring algorithm. It can't weigh intangibles only you can: referrals, growth potential, role flexibility, hiring-team appetite for trainable candidates.

Output format check

Before submitting the resulting CV, verify it meets ATS-parser expectations:

  • Plain text or single-column document — no tables, columns, text boxes, or images.
  • Standard section headers only: Summary, Skills, Experience, Education, Certifications, Licenses.
  • Contact info in the body, not in a page header or footer.
  • Dates in MM/YYYY – MM/YYYY format (or YYYY – YYYY if month is unknown).
  • No [verify:] markers anywhere in the CV body — all moved to, or removed from, the Verification Queue.
  • No FIT BRIEF section anywhere in the submitted file — deleted in full after reading.
  • No VERIFICATION QUEUE section anywhere in the submitted file — deleted in full after resolving.
  • No emoji, icons, or non-ASCII bullets (• is fine; decorative glyphs are not).

Document format for submission

The prompt outputs plain text. For submission, paste into a .docx file (preferred — most ATSes parse Word natively) or save as a text-layer PDF (not image-based). Avoid .pages, .rtf, .odt, or scanned PDFs.

How the prompt is structured — and why

Each section of the prompt defends against a specific screening risk.

Section Defends against
TRUTH BOUNDARY Interview-stage collapse, reference-check failure, background-verification mismatch, ATS knock-out-question discrepancies.
INFERENCE MARKER Both fabrication and ATS regex breakage from inline brackets in title or metric fields.
FIT BRIEF Applying to wrong-fit roles, failing to anticipate the interviewer's #1 objection, underselling a real strength in the cover letter, and burning weeks on closeable gaps you didn't see.
STRUCTURE Legacy ATS parser failures (Workday, Taleo, iCIMS, Greenhouse, Lever, SuccessFactors, BambooHR) silently dropping sections.
Top-of-page-1 block Recruiter abandoning the CV in the six-second scan because no JD-relevant signal lands above the fold.
First scope bullet Hiring manager rejecting the CV at substantive read because role complexity is undefined.
KEYWORD DISCIPLINE Low ATS match score from missing JD terms in genuine experience.
OUTPUT Recruiters or ATSes choking on commentary, preamble, or diff markers.

Known limitations

  1. The prompt cannot manufacture qualifications. If your real experience doesn't match the JD, the output will reflect that — long Verification Queue, hedged Summary. That's a signal to address the gap, not a flaw in the prompt.
  2. ATS behavior varies. The prompt targets the dominant ATS patterns (Workday, Taleo, iCIMS, Greenhouse, Lever, SuccessFactors). Smaller proprietary systems and newer LLM-based screening tools may behave differently. The structural choices — plain text, standard headers, atomic date lines — are conservative across the field.
  3. Headline ↔ title trade-off. The prompt prefers the JD's verbatim role title in the headline only where truthful. If your record can't legitimately claim the target title (e.g., Staff RN applying for Nurse Manager), the headline becomes the closest truthful alternative — which costs an ATS exact-match hit. This is intentional; faking the title gets you eliminated at interview anyway.
  4. The prompt does not optimize for visual design. Output is plain, parseable, and scannable — not "beautiful." If you need a designed CV (e.g., for a creative-industry portfolio submission), use this prompt's output as the content, then port it to a designed template for the human submission while keeping the plain version for the ATS upload.
